## üèõÔ∏è Town Planning and Engineering Marvels

The IVC, particularly in major cities like **Harappa** and **Mohenjo-Daro**, demonstrated urban planning that was centuries ahead of its time.

### üß± Grid Pattern and Standardization
* **Grid Layout:** Cities were laid out on a precise **grid pattern**, with major streets running North-South and East-West, intersecting at perfect **right angles**. This divided the city into rectangular blocks, similar to modern city planning. 
* **Citadel and Lower Town:** Most large cities were split into two main sections:
    * The **Citadel** (on the west): A raised, smaller area often fortified, believed to house administrative buildings, granaries, and structures with ritual significance, like the Great Bath.
    * The **Lower Town** (on the east): The larger residential area where the general populace lived.
* **Standardized Bricks:** A remarkable feature was the use of **standardized fired bricks** (baked bricks) across all major sites, following a uniform dimension ratio of **1:2:4** (thickness:width:length). This suggests a centralized authority or a powerful, shared building tradition.

### üöΩ The Advanced Drainage System of Harappa
* **Sophistication:** The drainage and sewage systems were perhaps the civilization's greatest engineering feat. Nearly every house had its own bathing area and, in some cases, private toilets with flushing mechanisms.
* **Covered Drains:** Waste water from the houses flowed into a system of covered drains built beneath the streets. These main drains were made of baked bricks and often covered with stone slabs for periodic cleaning.
* **Public Health:** This emphasis on **sanitation and hygiene** suggests a high value placed on public health, indicating organized civic life and potentially a municipal body overseeing the infrastructure.

### üõÅ The Great Bath of Mohenjo-Daro
* **Structure:** The Great Bath is the earliest known public water tank in the ancient world. It is a large, rectangular tank (approx. $11.88 \text{ m} \times 7.01 \text{ m} \times 2.43 \text{ m}$ deep) located in the Citadel.
* **Waterproofing:** Its floor was made watertight using carefully fitted baked bricks set on edge with gypsum plaster, and a layer of **natural bitumen (tar)** was placed between the layers‚Äîan incredible feat of ancient engineering.
* **Purpose (Ritual):** Scholars widely agree that the Great Bath was likely used for **ritual bathing or ceremonial purposes** rather than everyday bathing. The presence of smaller rooms around it (possibly for changing clothes) and a large well to supply the water supports this theory, suggesting a spiritual significance of water, similar to later Hindu traditions.

## üìú Seals and the Undeciphered Script

### üì¶ Purpose of Seals
* **Material and Design:** Thousands of seals, mostly square, made of **steatite** (a soft stone), have been found. They typically feature an animal motif (like the famous **"Unicorn Bull"** or the **"Pashupati Seal"** depicting a figure in a yogic pose) and a line of script.
* **Function:** Their main uses were likely:
    1.  **Trade and Administration:** Used to stamp clay tags on bundled goods, acting as a signature, trademark, or proof of ownership/origin for long-distance trade, including with Mesopotamia.
    2.  **Amulets/Identity:** Some may have been worn as amulets, suggesting religious or personal identity significance.

### ‚ùì The Undeciphered Script
* **The Challenge:** The script remains the biggest **enigma** of the IVC and has not been deciphered despite numerous attempts.
* **Characteristics:**
    * It consists of over 400 distinct signs, which are often pictorial and abstract.
    * Inscriptions are **extremely short**, averaging about five signs, with the longest known having only 26 symbols. This brevity makes linguistic analysis (identifying grammar or syntax) nearly impossible.
    * There is **no known bilingual inscription** (the Harappan equivalent of the Rosetta Stone) that could link the script to a known language.
* **Direction:** The writing direction is generally believed to be **right-to-left**, based on signs being compressed on the left edge of the seal, as if the writer ran out of space.

## üìâ Decline and Legacy

The IVC thrived in its Mature Harappan phase (c. 2600‚Äì1900 BCE) but began a period of decline (**Late Harappan Phase**) around 1900 BCE.

* **Decline Theories:** The decline was likely not a single catastrophic event but a complex process due to multiple factors:
    * **Climate Change (Most Accepted):** Prolonged droughts and a steady weakening of the monsoon system, which led to the drying up of the Ghaggar-Hakra river system (often identified with the Vedic Saraswati). This water scarcity severely impacted agriculture.
    * **Ecological Imbalance:** Over-cultivation, over-grazing, and excessive deforestation near the cities.
    * **Tectonic Activity:** Possible tectonic shifts that caused floods or diverted river courses, crippling water-based trade.
* **Transformation:** The decline led to a process of **de-urbanization**, where people slowly abandoned the large, complex cities like Mohenjo-Daro and migrated eastward toward the Ganges plain. The distinct Harappan features‚Äîstandardized bricks, seals, writing, and great public structures‚Äîeventually disappeared.

The Indus Valley Civilization provides a fascinating case study in **advanced urban planning, sophisticated water management, and the fragility of complex societies** in the face of climate change.
